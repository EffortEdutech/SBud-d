# AI Study Buddy

# Volume H10 – Study Groups & Knowledge Sharing Experience

**Version:** 1.0 (Architecture Freeze)

---

# 1. Introduction

The Study Groups & Knowledge Sharing Experience defines how students collaborate, share resources, and learn together within AI Study Buddy.

University students naturally learn through communities:

- Classmates.
- Course groups.
- Study partners.
- Academic communities.

AI Study Buddy provides a structured environment where collaboration can happen while preserving each student's personal learning identity.

---

# 2. Collaboration Philosophy

The experience follows these principles.

---

## Personal First

Every student maintains their own:

- Learning history.
- PLKG.
- Knowledge progress.

Shared resources do not replace personal understanding.

---

## Collaborative Growth

Students benefit from collective learning resources.

---

## Quality Over Quantity

The goal is useful academic collaboration, not endless content sharing.

---

## Controlled Sharing

Students control what they contribute and access.

---

# 3. Study Group Model

A Study Group represents a learning community.

Examples:

- Same subject students.
- Same programme students.
- Semester cohorts.
- Project groups.

---

# 4. Group Subscription Concept

AI Study Buddy supports group-based subscription models.

Example:

Individual subscription:

Student subscribes independently.

---

Group subscription:

Students from the same course form a learning group.

Benefits:

- Discounted subscription.
- Shared resources.
- Collaborative learning.
- Group knowledge environment.

---

# 5. Group Creation Experience

A student may:

- Create a study group.
- Invite classmates.
- Join an existing group.

Group information:

- Group name.
- Subject.
- Semester.
- Members.
- Shared resources.

---

# 6. Course-Based Study Groups

The strongest group experience is connected to subjects.

Example:

"Computer Science Year 1 - Data Structures"

Members share:

- Lecture notes.
- Study resources.
- Discussions.

---

# 7. Shared Knowledge Experience

Shared resources may include:

- Lecture notes.
- Summaries.
- Learning materials.
- Practice questions.
- Discussion insights.

Shared resources remain connected to their academic context.

---

# 8. Personal Knowledge vs Shared Knowledge

The system maintains separation.

Structure:

``` id="j3mv6d"
Shared Knowledge

↓

Student Reviews

↓

Student Understanding

↓

Personal PLKG Update
```

A shared note does not automatically become personal knowledge.

The student must learn and understand.

---

# 9. Resource Contribution Experience

Students may contribute:

- Notes.
- Explanations.
- Study materials.

The system may track:

- Source.
- Contributor.
- Subject relevance.

---

# 10. BLIE in Study Groups

BLIE can support collaborative learning.

Examples:

- Explain shared materials.
- Summarise discussions.
- Identify related concepts.
- Help resolve questions.

BLIE remains subject-aware.

---

# 11. Group Discussion Experience

Discussions should focus on learning.

Possible interactions:

- Questions.
- Explanations.
- Concept discussions.
- Study planning.

The objective is academic improvement.

---

# 12. Knowledge Quality Experience

Shared resources should maintain quality.

Possible indicators:

- Source information.
- Community feedback.
- Academic relevance.

Students should understand that shared content may require verification.

---

# 13. Privacy & Permission Model

Students control:

- What they share.
- Who can access resources.
- Group membership.

Personal learning data remains private by default.

---

# 14. Group Learning Progress

Groups may view:

- Shared activity.
- Resource contribution.
- Learning milestones.

However:

Individual mastery remains private.

---

# 15. Group Experience States

---

## Joined

---

## Active

Students collaborate.

---

## Archived

Completed courses remain accessible as history.

---

# 16. Offline Experience

Offline group access:

Available:

- Previously synchronized resources.
- Saved discussions.

Pending:

- New messages.
- New shared resources.

The group model supports sustainable growth.

Possible subscription structure:

## Individual Plan

Personal AI Study Buddy.

---

## Group Plan

Shared academic environment.

Benefits:

- Lower cost per student.
- Group collaboration.
- Shared learning resources.

---

## Future Expansion

Potential future academic communities may develop without changing the core architecture.

---

# 18. Architecture Freeze

The Study Groups & Knowledge Sharing Experience establishes the collaborative learning layer of AI Study Buddy.

The system shall:

1. Preserve individual learning ownership.
2. Enable structured collaboration.
3. Support course-based communities.
4. Allow group subscription models.
5. Maintain separation between shared resources and personal knowledge.
6. Enhance learning through collaboration.

AI Study Buddy remains a personal learning companion, strengthened by meaningful communities.
