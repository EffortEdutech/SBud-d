# AI Study Buddy

# Volume H5 – Subject Workspace Experience

**Version:** 1.0 (Architecture Freeze)

---

# 1. Introduction

The Subject Workspace defines the primary academic environment for each university subject.

Every enrolled subject becomes an intelligent learning space connected to:

- Curriculum structure.
- Student materials.
- BLIE assistance.
- Personal Learning Knowledge Graph (PLKG).
- Learning progress.

The Subject Workspace answers:

> "How can I understand and master this subject?"

---

# 2. Subject Workspace Philosophy

The Subject Workspace follows these principles.

## Subject-Centred Learning

Learning activities are organised around academic subjects.

---

## Knowledge Over Storage

Materials are transformed into understanding.

The workspace is not a file repository.

---

## Context-Aware Intelligence

BLIE understands the current subject context.

---

## Progressive Mastery

Students move from:

Introduction

↓

Understanding

↓

Practice

↓

Mastery

---

# 3. Subject Workspace Structure

The subject hierarchy:

```
Subject

├── Overview

├── Topics

├── Materials

├── Study Preparation

├── Learning Sessions

├── Revision

├── Assessment

├── Knowledge Map

└── Progress
```

---

# 4. Subject Overview

Purpose:

Provide immediate understanding of the subject.

Displays:

- Subject name.
- Subject code.
- Semester.
- Lecturer information (optional).
- Progress status.
- Current learning focus.

Example:

```
Data Structures

Current Topic:
Trees

Learning Status:
Developing Understanding
```

---

# 5. Topic Structure

Topics represent the learning journey within a subject.

Each topic contains:

- Topic overview.
- Required knowledge.
- Learning materials.
- Related concepts.
- BLIE support.
- Progress status.

Topics become nodes within the PLKG.

---

# 6. Topic Learning Experience

For each topic, students can:

- Read explanations.
- Ask BLIE questions.
- Review materials.
- Explore related concepts.
- Practice understanding.

The objective is comprehension, not information consumption.

---

# 7. Materials Experience

Materials include:

- Lecture notes.
- PDF documents.
- Images.
- Slides.
- References.
- Personal notes.

Materials are processed into structured knowledge.

The student sees both:

Original Material

+

Extracted Learning Knowledge

---

# 8. Document-to-Knowledge Journey

The experience:

```
Upload Material

↓

AI Processing

↓

Information Extraction

↓

Concept Identification

↓

Knowledge Mapping

↓

PLKG Update
```

The student gradually builds a personal academic knowledge base.

---

# 9. BLIE Inside Subject Workspace

BLIE operates with subject awareness.

Example:

Instead of:

"Explain recursion"

BLIE understands:

"Explain recursion based on my Data Structures subject."

BLIE can assist with:

- Concept explanations.
- Topic preparation.
- Revision.
- Practice questions.
- Knowledge connections.

---

# 10. Study Preparation Experience

Before a topic begins:

BLIE helps students prepare.

Example:

Upcoming Topic:

"Neural Networks"

Preparation:

- Review mathematics foundations.
- Understand basic concepts.
- Learn required terminology.

The goal:

Students arrive prepared.

---

# 11. Learning Session Experience

Students can enter focused learning sessions.

A session may include:

- Topic explanation.
- Guided learning.
- Questions.
- Practice.
- Summary.

The session contributes to PLKG growth.

---

# 12. Revision Experience

Revision connects previous learning.

Students can:

- Review topics.
- Identify weak areas.
- Ask BLIE for clarification.
- Practice understanding.

Revision is personalised based on knowledge state.

---

# 13. Assessment Support

The Subject Workspace supports preparation for:

- Quizzes.
- Assignments.
- Examinations.

Features include:

- Practice questions.
- Concept review.
- Knowledge gap identification.

The objective is understanding, not memorisation only.

---

# 14. Subject Knowledge Map

Each subject has a connected knowledge view.

Students can see:

- Topics.
- Relationships.
- Learning progress.
- Missing foundations.

The subject knowledge map contributes to the overall PLKG.

---

# 15. Subject Progress

Progress should represent understanding.

Possible indicators:

- Topics introduced.
- Topics understood.
- Topics requiring review.
- Mastery development.

Completion alone is not sufficient.

---

# 16. Collaboration Readiness

The Subject Workspace can later support:

- Shared resources.
- Study groups.
- Collaborative knowledge.

However, personal learning remains the foundation.

---

# 17. Offline Experience

Offline subject access includes:

Available:

- Downloaded materials.
- Previous explanations.
- Local knowledge.

Pending:

- New AI processing.
- Cloud synchronization.

The student remains productive offline.

---

# 18. Subject Workspace States

Required states:

## New Subject

"Your learning space is ready."

## Processing Materials

"AI is organising your knowledge."

## Active Learning

"Continue your progress."

## Completed Subject

"Your knowledge has been added to your academic journey."

---

# 19. Architecture Freeze

The Subject Workspace establishes the main academic environment of AI Study Buddy.

The workspace shall:

1. Organise learning around subjects.
2. Transform materials into structured knowledge.
3. Provide subject-aware BLIE assistance.
4. Connect learning activities to PLKG.
5. Support preparation, learning, revision, and assessment.
6. Grow with the student throughout university.

The Subject Workspace is the bridge between academic curriculum and personal intelligence.
